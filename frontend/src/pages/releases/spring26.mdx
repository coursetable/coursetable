# CourseTable 2025 + Spring 2026 Release Notes

_2026-04-27_

Hi everyone\!

We hope you've had a great semester and a productive start to reading week\! This school year was another successful year for CourseTable. We welcomed several new team members and shipped many new features.

Last January, [we documented what we did in the fall 2024 semester](/releases/fall24), and since then we have made many new changes. We document the most notable changes here outlined below. Please also [let us know](https://feedback.coursetable.com/) what you want to see next\!

## Download calendar as PNG

You can now download your calendar as a PNG, making it even simpler to send your classes to your friends\!

This feature is contributed by Aryan Sharma ([@aryans-15](https://github.com/aryans-15)).

## Clear worksheet

You can now clear your whole worksheet at once: open worksheet settings and use “Clear All Classes”. All courses are removed in one go, and you'll see a short confirmation when it's done.

This feature is contributed by Reyansh Bahl ([@reybahl](https://github.com/reybahl)).

## Add identifying information to exotic worksheets

When you open a shared worksheet link, you can now see the worksheet's name and who created it.

This feature is contributed by Reyansh Bahl ([@reybahl](https://github.com/reybahl)).

## Show current time indicator on worksheet calendar

We now display a current time indicator on the calendar view, similar to Google Calendar, so that you know what's coming up next on your schedule\!

This feature is contributed by Aryan Sharma ([@aryans-15](https://github.com/aryans-15)).

## Map for classes

Knowing where courses are located on Yale's campus can be tricky, especially for first-years and transfers. To ease this friction, we've added a map that displays all your worksheet's classes. Hovering over courses in the right panel lights up locations in orange, and clicking on a pin opens a popup showing all relevant courses at that given location. The map integrates seamlessly with dark mode and is mobile-friendly.

This feature is contributed by Yavin Fickel ([@yav-fi](https://github.com/yav-fi)).

## Cross-listed friends

We now show friends enrolled in cross-listed versions of a course directly in the course modal, so you no longer need to click through each cross-listing to see who's taking it\!

This feature is contributed by William Feng ([@WFeng7](https://github.com/WFeng7)).

## Calendar toggle + related UI updates

This feature is contributed by Yanga Booker.

## Prompt latest offering for historical course adds

When adding a course from a past semester, we ask you if you'd like to add the latest offering of the course instead; that way, you don't accidentally save outdated classes.

This feature is contributed by Aryan Sharma ([@aryans-15](https://github.com/aryans-15)).

## Private worksheet indicator

This feature is contributed by Brent Hu.

## Show walking times on calendar

Building on our focus to make Yale courses easier to navigate and plan for, we've implemented walking times displayed for back-to-back classes. To show our best guess efficiently and intelligently, we draw from walking time combinations pre-calculated with Google Maps API. We also warn you if your walk time exceeds the gap between classes, helping you build a feasible worksheet. Clicking on walk-time chips gives more details on our calculation.

This feature is contributed by Yavin Fickel ([@yav-fi](https://github.com/yav-fi)).

## Import worksheet

You can import a schedule from a shared worksheet URL into one of your worksheets: choose the destination worksheet, and the courses are added in bulk. If a friend or DUS exports and shares a worksheet with you, you can easily bring those courses into your worksheet.

This feature is contributed by Reyansh Bahl ([@reybahl](https://github.com/reybahl)).

## Profile pages

CourseTable now has profile pages\! [Your own profile page](/profile) is a central hub to manage your account info, friends, and privacy settings, with per-field controls allowing you to set each detail to public, friends-only, or private. Other Yalies can view your public profile at the `/u/:netId` path, seeing only what you've chosen to share (by default, your details are private)\!

This feature is contributed by Aryan Sharma ([@aryans-15](https://github.com/aryans-15)).

## Discussion section indicator in worksheet

This feature is contributed by Brent Hu.

## Aggregate ratings on course modal

We now show overall, workload, and professor ratings directly in the course modal, so you can view aggregate ratings without clicking away\!

This feature is contributed by William Feng ([@WFeng7](https://github.com/WFeng7)).

## Wishlists

Wishlists let you save courses you're interested in without committing them to a worksheet. They're a lightweight way to track possibilities as you explore the course catalog or save courses for future seasons\!

This feature is contributed by Ben Xu ([@benzuzu](https://github.com/benzuzu)), Humphrey Xu ([@Etherite1](https://github.com/Etherite1)), Aryan Sharma ([@aryans-15](https://github.com/aryans-15)), and Reyansh Bahl ([@reybahl](https://github.com/reybahl)).

## AI course evaluation summaries

We've begun rolling out summaries of course evaluations to make it easier to get an at-a-glance overview of the evaluation narratives. We started with fall 2025 and will be adding summaries for other seasons soon\!

This feature is contributed by Eli Bouganim ([@eliboug](https://github.com/eliboug)) and Reyansh Bahl ([@reybahl](https://github.com/reybahl)).

## Show number of main worksheets with course

This feature is contributed by Michael Canudas ([@michaelcanudas](https://github.com/michaelcanudas)).

## Ferry locations logic updates

After Yale Course Search restricted locations to signed-in users, we had to rethink how Ferry, our scraper that runs daily to keep course data up to date, ingests room locations (including a larger data migration: either freezing locations to a known snapshot or using a short-lived session to refresh them) so we stay aligned with YCS. On CourseTable we now only expose course locations to users who have evaluations access.

This feature is contributed by Reyansh Bahl ([@reybahl](https://github.com/reybahl)).

## Bug fixes, polishes, infra

- CourseTable no longer remembers which friend's profile you had open across full-page reloads, so a refresh or a later visit won't land you on someone else's profile by surprise. Contributed by Reyansh Bahl ([@reybahl](https://github.com/reybahl)).
- For worksheet export as calendar, if Google stops allowing the export (for example when access expired), you'll be nudged to sign in to Google again and can finish the export without getting stuck in a dead end. Contributed by Reyansh Bahl ([@reybahl](https://github.com/reybahl)).
- The course share control is now a small menu: you can copy just the course code or just the link, depending on what you want to paste. Contributed by Eli Bouganim ([@eliboug](https://github.com/eliboug)).
- The shareable link you copy points to the course in the catalog, even if you had the course modal open from another page (like your worksheet). Contributed by Eli Bouganim ([@eliboug](https://github.com/eliboug)).
- We upgraded many of our core libraries: React 19, React Router 7, Vite 6, and Zustand 5. Contributed by Sida Chen ([@Josh-Cena](https://github.com/josh-cena)) and Reyansh Bahl ([@reybahl](https://github.com/reybahl)).
- If the CourseTable maintainers had to manually turn on evaluations access for your account, that access stays put when you log in instead of sometimes being lost on the next sign-in. Contributed by Reyansh Bahl ([@reybahl](https://github.com/reybahl)).
- We made the course modal overview a bit easier to read: there's more breathing room between the two main columns on larger screens. Contributed by William Feng ([@WFeng7](https://github.com/WFeng7)).
- We updated the library we use to display the success/error notifications (“toasts”) in the top right of the app. Contributed by Reyansh Bahl ([@reybahl](https://github.com/reybahl)).
- We've finished migrating global app state from React Context to Zustand to make state management more predictable and maintainable across our codebase. Contributed by Neil Song ([@neilsong](https://github.com/neilsong)) and Aryan Sharma ([@aryans-15](https://github.com/aryans-15)).
- Over the summer, Yale Course Search changed how buildings, rooms, and meeting rows are represented, while our location and course meeting tables and sync logic still assumed the old shape. That mismatch caused location rows to get wiped or rewritten, bad foreign keys, and noisy or duplicate course meeting rows per run. We realigned the importer and DB sync (updating the incremental course meetings logic, resolving location IDs from building+room, deduping, and stricter diff/equality rules) so schedules and rooms stay consistent with what Yale publishes, addressing a locations issue that showed up over the summer. This was contributed by Reyansh Bahl ([@reybahl](https://github.com/reybahl)) and Neil Song ([@neilsong](https://github.com/neilsong)).
